Privacy Policy

This privacy policy governs your use of the mobile application Mathdoku: Logic Grid Puzzle (application identifier: com.mathdoku.light) created by neuraldevarc.

Introduction

We are committed to protecting and respecting your privacy. This privacy policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your options regarding your personal data when you play Mathdoku.

By downloading, installing, and using the application, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

Information Collection and Use

For a better experience, while using our Service, we may require you to provide us with certain personally identifiable information. The information that we request will be retained on your device and is not collected by us in any way.

However, the application does utilize third-party services that may collect information used to identify you. Specifically, this application uses advertisement SDKs and consent tools to serve advertisements and manage privacy regulations.

Permissions requested by the application include:

  • INTERNET: Required to fetch puzzle configurations, load advertisements, and retrieve user consent status.
  • ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE: Required to check if a network connection is active before requesting advertisements or SDK data.

Log Data & Cookies

Whenever you use our Service, in a case of an error in the app we collect data and information (through third-party products) on your phone called Log Data. This Log Data may include information such as your device Internet Protocol (“IP”) address, device name, operating system version, the configuration of the app when utilizing our Service, the time and date of your use of the Service, and other statistics.

Cookies are files with a small amount of data that are commonly used as anonymous unique identifiers. These are sent to your browser from the websites that you visit and are stored on your device's internal memory. This Service does not use these "cookies" explicitly. However, the app may use third-party code and libraries that use "cookies" to collect information and improve their services.

GDPR & CCPA/CPRA Rights

If you reside in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or California, you have specific data protection rights under regulations such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):

  • The right to access: You have the right to request copies of your personal data.
  • The right to rectification: You have the right to request that we correct any information you believe is inaccurate.
  • The right to erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"): You have the right to request that we erase your personal data under certain conditions.
  • The right to opt-out of ad personalization: You can configure your choices in the in-game consent settings or revoke your consent at any time.

In Mathdoku, you can manage your preferences or change your consent choices at any time by going to the Settings screen in the game and choosing to adjust your Privacy Consent Settings. This will re-trigger the Google User Messaging Platform (UMP) dialog.

Children's Privacy (COPPA)

These Services do not address anyone under the age of 13 (or under the age of 16 in the EEA). We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from children under 13. In the case we discover that a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, we immediately delete this from our servers. If you are a parent or guardian and you are aware that your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us so that we will be able to take necessary actions.

Data Retention & Deletion Policy

Since Mathdoku is a fully offline-first game, we do not host or store your game data or personal details on any external databases or cloud servers. All level progression and setting choices are stored strictly locally on your own device's internal memory (via Android SharedPreferences/DataStore).

To completely delete your local game data: You can go to your Android device settings, select Apps > Mathdoku > Storage, and click Clear Storage/Data. This will permanently wipe all local save states, settings, and progress from your device.

Security of Data

We value your trust in providing us your personal information, thus we are striving to use commercially acceptable means of protecting it. But remember that no method of transmission over the internet, or method of electronic storage is 100% secure and reliable, and we cannot guarantee its absolute security.
